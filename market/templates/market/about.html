The Origin of LinkHash

LinkHash originally launched as a crypto reward-based task platform where users earned rewards by completing simple and verifiable tasks. Inspired by how the Bitcoin network rewards miners for discovering valid hashes through computation, LinkHash was designed to mirror that concept in the online world. Instead of mining blocks, users complete meaningful online actions and are rewarded with cryptocurrency. This idea led to the creation of the name “LinkHash”, combining “Link” (representing connections, actions, and engagement across the web) and “Hash” (symbolizing verification, proof, and crypto-native rewards).

Example tasks included:
  • Following specific social media accounts
  • Posting themed content on their own feeds
  • Participating in surveys
  • Downloading apps and leaving reviews
After users submitted proof, LinkHash’s manual and automated verification systems reviewed submissions. Verified users received rewards in cryptocurrencies such as USDT and SOL.

From day one, LinkHash aimed to become a Web3-native reward-driven marketing platform.

How LinkHash Has Evolved

LinkHash has evolved far beyond task-based rewards. Today, it operates as a full-stack growth engine for DEX projects.
Promotion Support
Projects that promote their DEX/token via LinkHash receive support across LinkHash channels, along with social-driven amplification and distribution.
Market Making & Liquidity
The LinkHash team can support market making activities such as liquidity expansion, post-launch volume growth, and graduation strategy support.
End-to-End Incubation
From day one, LinkHash can help founders set up a project end-to-end: pump.fun / four.meme launch setup, X & Telegram setup, and early narrative positioning.
A Growth Marketplace
LinkHash is evolving into a permissionless marketplace where campaigns, incentives, and distribution can be launched and scaled with minimal friction.

Campaign System Evolution

The campaign system is being redesigned so that campaigns are not only opened by administrators.

Instead, LinkHash participants will be able to create campaigns autonomously by paying a small fee in LinkHash tokens (LHX) or SOL. This enables a more permissionless and scalable campaign marketplace. While crypto promotion will be the primary use case, LinkHash ultimately serves as a permissionless campaign and advertising infrastructure for any project seeking verified engagement.
